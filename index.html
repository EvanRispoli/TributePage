Nikola Tesla

The Genius Inventor and Engineer

A Brief Overview of Tesla's Life

  • 1856 - Born in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia)
  • 1884 - Immigrates to the United States to work with Thomas Edison, but later becomes rivals due to differences in AC and DC electricity systems.
  • 1888 - Patents the alternating current (AC) induction motor and transformer, revolutionizing the electrical industry.
  • 1891 - Demonstrates wireless transmission of electricity, pioneering radio waves and wireless communication.
  • 1893 - Successfully lights up the Chicago World's Fair using AC power, proving the superiority of AC over DC.
  • 1896 - Begins construction of Wardenclyffe Tower, intended for wireless communication and transmission of electrical power.
  • 1943 - Passes away in New York City, leaving behind a legacy of innovation and scientific achievement.
